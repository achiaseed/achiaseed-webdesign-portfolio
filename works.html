Missions Conference 2021: case study

(Sept 2020-Mar 2021)


Biola University holds an annual school-wide conference, inviting speakers from all over to share their experiences from missions work and to encourage students to be bold. Due to the global pandemic, Missions Conference 2021 moved to an online virutal platform to be accessible to everyone.

As the UX/UI designer of this product, my purpose was to bridge the gap of developers and designers. I ensured the designers got their ideas across and the developers could manifest those into the product. My responsibilities consisted of creating the general UI of the website to pass on to our development team. These neatly-designed pages allowed users to navigate the virtual conference with ease and readability. In addition, I created wireframes and mock prototypes to guide the developers in how best to achieve good user flow. Each screen differed in its purpose and functionality from creating global awarness on serious topics, to playing guest speaker videos, even listing out organizations and their upcoming mission trips.

With the magnitude of information normally conveyed in-person, we needed to create a clean and neat design that followed accessbility guidelines, while still compelling our target audience to be inspired and take action.

Cozy

Sept 2020-present
The number of people in the world struggling with anxiety today is unprecedent. Even I struggle with anxiety on a daily basis. Whenever I feel my anxiety rise, I try to find a safe haven which often is home for me to wait out the troubling waves. It has always been my desire to create a small app to help those like me calm down and have that sense of "it will be okay, you're not alone, you did well, and I am proud of you" right at their fingertips.

The premises of Cozy is the user has the ability to interact with wildlife in their backyard. Depending on the presence of food or time of day, different animals will appear. I hope to implement the calming activity of garden and baking into the game mechanics as well as calming sound effects like a water fountain, rain, a fireplace, rustling of leaves, and more.

CryoFind

Feb. 2020 - June 2020
Cryotherapy is a recent route of physical therapy; however, it is not well known to the public. Agency 41 created an app called CryoFind to help wellness centers who supply cryotherapy machines to reach a wider audience and help people live full and healthy lives. CryoFind's target audience is intended for the general public and physical therapy centers with cryogenic machines.

Since the app is still in the initial creation stage, my task was to create marketing pages for CryoFind and conduct user research on cryotherapy business owners. This consisted of setting up video calls with owners and documenting their difficulties in reaching wider audiences, as well as noting what resources they found helpful. TransportOp

Spr. 2020
TransportOp is a program based on the research of Dr. Shieu-Hong Lin. Its purpose is to find the optimal route for drivers to take while fueling with least amount of gas to complete the journey. We used the algorithm developed by Dr. Lin to create a front-end interface and database for potential drivers; TransportOp helps drivers successfully get to their destinations, and assists gas station owners in managing their gas prices.

I was in charge of creating a small-scaled design system (shown below) and the webpage application. Our target users consisted of truck drivers and gas station owners; therefore, two log-in type views were created to serve the symbiotic relationship.

Campaign Buddy

Spr. 2019
I worked on the UX/UI of Campaign Buddy, which is designed to help Dungeons and Dragons game masters organize and run their campaigns efficiently. This program was created by myself and five other students as our senior software engineering project. Our program assumes that our users understand how to operate a campaign within the rules of D&D. Our aim in designing the interface was to replicate the look and feel of the official D&D Player’s Handbook, to evoke familiarity with our users while they interact with our program. Instead of sifting through unorganized documents and breaking the flow of the game, users will be able to smoothly manage their projects with Campaign Buddy.

Responsibilities: created the design system, generated wireframes, coded the dice roll feature.

SGA Connect

September 2017 - December 2017
This semester long project was assigned during my User Interface and Design course. The goal was to create a mock program to help Biola’s student government reach their students effectively. We worked with NetBeans and Java Swing to create a desktop program. I mainly worked on the UX/UI of the project, though designs were limited due to the constraints of the program and limited user testing. User testing included creating forms for our testers to give feedback, gathering testers, and conducting the tests. It also involved observing which features were easily identified by users, and which features were more difficult to locate.

SGA connect was modeled to look similar to “Canvas,” a website used by a wide population of students and professors to view grades, assignments, files, and syllabi. Since users associate the Canvas interface with academics, our application will evoke the same, familiar academic atmosphere in the minds of users as they navigate it.

Responsibilities: created the design system, generated wireframes, conducted user testing.
